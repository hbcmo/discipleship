# Harmony Baptist Church - Comprehensive Discipleship Curriculum
## From New Convert to Ministry Leader

**Framework Integration:**
- Harmony Baptist Church Membership Covenant
- Baptist Faith and Message 2000
- 2nd London Baptist Confession (1689)

---

## PHASE 1: GOSPEL FOUNDATION
**Duration:** 4 weeks | **Target Audience:** New believers, recent converts

### Core Objectives
- Understand the Gospel message
- Confirm personal salvation experience
- Begin personal relationship with Christ

### Doctrinal Focus
- **BFM 2000:** Section IV (Salvation) - The work of Christ on the cross and personal faith
- **2nd London Confession:** Chapter 8 (Christ the Mediator), Chapter 11 (Justification)
- **HBC Covenant:** Statement of Basic Belief points 5-7 (sin, penalty, Christ's substitutionary death)

### Weekly Study Breakdown

**Week 1: The Gospel Story**
- Scripture: Romans 3:21-26, 1 Corinthians 15:1-4
- Core Questions:
  1. What does it mean that Christ died "for me"?
  2. How does understanding substitution change my view of the cross?
  3. What was my spiritual condition before trusting Christ?
- Spiritual Practice: Daily scripture reading (15 min), written prayer response
- Habits to Develop: Daily prayer, begin daily Bible reading

**Week 2: Justification and Peace**
- Scripture: Romans 5:1-11, 2 Corinthians 5:17-21
- Core Questions:
  1. How does justification differ from feeling guilty?
  2. What does it mean to be "declared righteous"?
  3. Why does Paul emphasize "peace with God"?
- Spiritual Practice: Memorize Romans 3:22, journaling about areas of doubt
- Habits to Develop: Weekly church attendance, connect with one Christian friend

**Week 3: Regeneration - The New Birth**
- Scripture: John 3:1-8, 2 Corinthians 5:17, Titus 3:4-7
- Core Questions:
  1. How do I know I've been "born again"?
  2. What changes should I expect in my desires and values?
  3. What does it mean to be "a new creation"?
- Spiritual Practice: Life inventory - areas of change since conversion
- Habits to Develop: Join a small group or connect with discipler weekly

**Week 4: Assurance and Security**
- Scripture: 1 John 5:11-13, Romans 8:31-39, John 10:27-30
- Core Questions:
  1. How certain am I that I belong to Christ?
  2. What does God's sovereignty mean for my security?
  3. Can a true believer lose their salvation?
- Spiritual Practice: Read testimony of faith, write personal testimony outline
- Habits to Develop: Prayer, worship, connection to body of Christ

### Assessment
- Articulate the Gospel message in simple terms
- Share personal testimony (written or verbal)
- Commit to Phase 2

## PHASE 2: BIBLICAL FOUNDATIONS
**Duration:** 5 weeks | **Target Audience:** All new members

### Core Objectives
- Understand biblical authority and canon
- Develop Bible study skills
- Establish spiritual disciplines

### Doctrinal Focus
- **BFM 2000:** Section I (The Scriptures)
- **2nd London Confession:** Chapter 1 (Holy Scriptures)
- **HBC Covenant:** Basic Belief point 1 (Scripture authority and sufficiency)

### Weekly Study Breakdown

**Week 1: Why the Bible Matters**
- Scripture: 2 Timothy 3:14-17, 2 Peter 1:20-21, Psalm 119:1-16
- Core Questions:
  1. How do I know the Bible is God's Word?
  2. What does "inspiration" mean?
  3. Why should I submit to biblical authority in my life?
- Spiritual Practice: Read entire Psalm 119, identify verses about God's Word
- Habits to Develop: Daily Bible reading (increase to 20-30 min)

**Week 2: The Old Testament Story**
- Scripture: 2 Timothy 3:15-17, Luke 24:25-27, Romans 15:4
- Core Questions:
  1. How does the Old Testament connect to Jesus?
  2. What can I learn from Old Testament believers?
  3. How do Old Testament laws apply today?
- Spiritual Practice: Create a one-page timeline of salvation history
- Habits to Develop: Bible reading in Old Testament books

**Week 3: The Gospel Accounts**
- Scripture: Matthew 28:19-20, John 20:31, Luke 1:1-4
- Core Questions:
  1. Why four Gospels? What's unique about each?
  2. How are the Gospels both historical and theological?
  3. What should I do with Jesus's teachings?
- Spiritual Practice: Read one complete Gospel over the week
- Habits to Develop: Reflection writing after reading

**Week 4: The Epistles and Application**
- Scripture: Romans 1:1-7, 1 John 1:1-4, Hebrews 4:12
- Core Questions:
  1. How are the epistles different from Gospels?
  2. How do I apply Paul's letters to my life today?
  3. What's the difference between descriptive and prescriptive passages?
- Spiritual Practice: Study 1 John 1 in depth using SOAP method (Scripture, Observation, Application, Prayer)
- Habits to Develop: Personal Bible study notebook

**Week 5: Building a Scripture Habit**
- Scripture: Deuteronomy 6:4-9, Joshua 1:8, Psalm 1
- Core Questions:
  1. What's the difference between reading and meditation?
  2. How do I memorize scripture effectively?
  3. What does it look like to love God's Word?
- Spiritual Practice: Commit to memorizing Psalm 23 or John 1:1-3
- Habits to Develop: Establish consistent Bible reading plan

### Assessment
- Demonstrate basic Bible study skills
- Share one spiritual insight from personal Bible study
- Establish daily Bible reading habit
- Commit to Phase 3

## PHASE 3: DISCIPLESHIP BASICS & THE CHURCH
**Duration:** 5 weeks | **Target Audience:** All members

### Core Objectives
- Understand church nature, mission, and membership
- Develop community engagement
- Embrace covenantal responsibilities

### Doctrinal Focus
- **BFM 2000:** Section VI (The Church)
- **2nd London Confession:** Chapter 26 (The Church), Chapter 29 (Baptism)
- **HBC Covenant:** Entire document - both blessings and obligations of membership

### Weekly Study Breakdown

**Week 1: Why the Church Exists**
- Scripture: Matthew 16:18, 1 Corinthians 12:12-27, Ephesians 2:14-22
- Core Questions:
  1. What is the church's primary purpose?
  2. How does God display His glory through the church?
  3. What's my personal role in the church's mission?
- Spiritual Practice: Visit church history timeline, identify key moments
- Habits to Develop: Regular worship attendance, connect with 3 new people at church

**Week 2: The Body of Christ - Membership**
- Scripture: 1 Corinthians 12:12-27, Romans 12:3-8, Ephesians 4:4-16
- Core Questions:
  1. What does "body of Christ" mean practically?
  2. How do spiritual gifts equip the church?
  3. Why do I need other believers?
- Spiritual Practice: Spiritual gifts assessment, identify your gifting
- Habits to Develop: Volunteer for a church ministry

**Week 3: Church Leadership and Accountability**
- Scripture: Hebrews 13:17, 1 Thessalonians 5:12-13, 1 Timothy 3
- Core Questions:
  1. Who are the leaders of our church and what's their responsibility?
  2. Why should I submit to church leadership?
  3. What's the purpose of accountability?
- Spiritual Practice: Read Harmony's Statement of Faith, identify key doctrines
- Habits to Develop: Prayer for pastors and elders by name

**Week 4: The Covenantal Community**
- Scripture: Joshua 24:14-25, Nehemiah 10, Hebrews 10:24-25
- Core Questions:
  1. What does it mean to covenant with a church body?
  2. What are my obligations as a member?
  3. What can I expect from church leadership?
- Spiritual Practice: Read and sign Harmony's Membership Covenant
- Habits to Develop: Join a small group or discipleship group

**Week 5: Ordinances - Baptism and Communion**
- Scripture: Matthew 28:18-20, 1 Corinthians 11:23-26, Romans 6:1-11
- Core Questions:
  1. Why is baptism important?
  2. What does baptism symbolize about my faith?
  3. Why do we practice communion regularly?
- Spiritual Practice: Prepare for baptism (if not yet baptized) or reflection on baptismal vows
- Habits to Develop: Participate in communion with reflection and prayer

### Assessment
- Articulate understanding of church membership
- Sign Harmony's Membership Covenant (or reaffirm)
- Join a small group or ministry
- Commit to Phase 4

## PHASE 4: SANCTIFICATION & HOLINESS
**Duration:** 6 weeks | **Target Audience:** All maturing members

### Core Objectives
- Understand sanctification as progressive transformation
- Identify areas of personal holiness development
- Embrace covenant obligations regarding godly living

### Doctrinal Focus
- **BFM 2000:** Section IV.C (Sanctification)
- **2nd London Confession:** Chapter 13 (Sanctification)
- **HBC Covenant:** Obligations section 6 (walk in holiness) and section 8 (submit to discipline)

### Weekly Study Breakdown

**Week 1: The Nature of Sanctification**
- Scripture: 1 Corinthians 6:19-20, 1 Thessalonians 4:3-8, 1 Peter 1:14-16
- Core Questions:
  1. How does sanctification differ from justification?
  2. Is holiness attained or pursued?
  3. What's the Holy Spirit's role in my growth toward godliness?
- Spiritual Practice: Life audit - areas of growth and areas needing work
- Habits to Develop: Daily surrender prayer

**Week 2: Putting Off and Putting On**
- Scripture: Ephesians 4:25-32, Colossians 3:5-17, Romans 12:1-2
- Core Questions:
  1. What sinful patterns am I to "put to death"?
  2. What Christ-like character should I develop?
  3. How does transformation happen practically?
- Spiritual Practice: Identify one sin pattern to address, one virtue to develop
- Habits to Develop: Accountability partnership with someone in Phase 3+

**Week 3: Sexual Purity and Marriage**
- Scripture: 1 Corinthians 6:18-20, Hebrews 13:4, 1 Thessalonians 4:1-8
- Core Questions:
  1. What does biblical sexual ethics look like?
  2. How does purity honor God and myself?
  3. If married, how do I cherish my spouse? If single, how do I maintain purity?
- Spiritual Practice: Memorize 1 Corinthians 6:18-20, personal reflection on relationship boundaries
- Habits to Develop: Accountability for thought life and relationships

**Week 4: Freedom and Christian Conscience**
- Scripture: Romans 14:1-23, 1 Corinthians 8:1-13, Colossians 2:16-23
- Core Questions:
  1. What does Christian freedom mean?
  2. How do I know what's sin versus preference?
  3. When should I restrict my freedom for others' sake?
- Spiritual Practice: Examine personal practices - what's liberty? what's bondage?
- Habits to Develop: Seek counsel from discipler on conscience questions

**Week 5: Suffering, Trial, and Growth**
- Scripture: Romans 5:1-5, James 1:2-4, 1 Peter 1:3-7
- Core Questions:
  1. Why does God allow trials?
  2. How can suffering produce spiritual maturity?
  3. What's my responsibility during difficulty?
- Spiritual Practice: Reflect on a past trial and God's faithfulness
- Habits to Develop: Prayer during current struggles

**Week 6: The Disciplines of Grace**
- Scripture: 1 Timothy 4:7-8, 2 Timothy 2:3-6, Hebrews 12:1-2
- Core Questions:
  1. What spiritual disciplines strengthen faith?
  2. Why does discipline matter if God's grace is sufficient?
  3. What disciplines should I prioritize?
- Spiritual Practice: Design personal Rule of Life (prayer, Bible reading, fasting, service schedule)
- Habits to Develop: Implementation of personal spiritual disciplines

### Assessment
- Articulate a biblical understanding of sanctification
- Share one area of growth and one area being addressed
- Join or deepen accountability relationships
- Commit to Phase 5

## PHASE 5: SPIRITUAL LEADERSHIP ESSENTIALS
**Duration:** 6 weeks | **Target Audience:** Those showing leadership readiness

### Core Objectives
- Understand biblical leadership model
- Develop servant leadership mentality
- Prepare for small group or ministry leadership

### Doctrinal Focus
- **BFM 2000:** Section VI (Church leadership qualifications)
- **2nd London Confession:** Chapter 26 (Church officers and governance)
- **HBC Covenant:** Elder and deacon obligations (can be adapted for small group leaders)

### Weekly Study Breakdown

**Week 1: Servant Leadership Model**
- Scripture: Matthew 20:24-28, Mark 10:42-45, John 13:1-17
- Core Questions:
  1. How did Jesus model leadership?
  2. What's the difference between worldly and kingdom leadership?
  3. Why is servanthood the foundation of Christian leadership?
- Spiritual Practice: Identify someone who models servant leadership; observe their practices
- Habits to Develop: Service to those in need

**Week 2: Character Before Competence**
- Scripture: 1 Timothy 3:1-13, Titus 1:5-9, 1 Peter 5:1-4
- Core Questions:
  1. What character qualities does leadership require?
  2. Why is character more important than ability?
  3. Am I developing these qualities?
- Spiritual Practice: Self-assessment against 1 Timothy 3 qualifications
- Habits to Develop: Confession of character weaknesses, prayer for growth

**Week 3: Vision Casting and Direction**
- Scripture: Proverbs 29:18, Habakkuk 2:2-3, Nehemiah 2:11-18
- Core Questions:
  1. How does a leader communicate vision?
  2. How does vision connect to God's purpose?
  3. What's my vision for the group/ministry I lead?
- Spiritual Practice: Write a mission/vision statement for area of leadership
- Habits to Develop: Regular prayer for clarity and direction

**Week 4: Multiplication and Delegation**
- Scripture: 2 Timothy 2:2, Ephesians 4:11-13, Matthew 28:19-20
- Core Questions:
  1. What does it mean to multiply leaders?
  2. Why is delegation essential to kingdom impact?
  3. How do I empower others?
- Spiritual Practice: Identify someone to invest in; create mentoring plan
- Habits to Develop: Begin informal mentoring relationship

**Week 5: Conflict Resolution and Accountability**
- Scripture: Matthew 18:15-20, Ephesians 4:1-6, 2 Timothy 2:24-26
- Core Questions:
  1. How should believers handle conflict?
  2. What's the leader's role in reconciliation?
  3. How do I hold people accountable in love?
- Spiritual Practice: Case study analysis of biblical conflict resolution
- Habits to Develop: Practice difficult conversation with discipler feedback

**Week 6: Leading with Wisdom and Prayer**
- Scripture: James 1:5, James 3:13-18, Philippians 4:4-7
- Core Questions:
  1. How do I seek God's wisdom for decisions?
  2. What's the relationship between prayer and leadership?
  3. How do I discern between my will and God's?
- Spiritual Practice: Bring a real leadership decision to prayer; journal the process
- Habits to Develop: Prayer-focused decision making in leadership

### Assessment
- Demonstrate servant leadership heart
- Show character growth in assessed areas
- Present mentoring plan for someone you'll invest in
- Be formally recognized in a ministry leadership role
- Commit to Phase 6

## PHASE 6: DOCTRINAL DEPTH & CHRISTIAN THINKING
**Duration:** 8 weeks | **Target Audience:** Leaders and mature believers

### Core Objectives
- Understand Baptist theological heritage
- Develop doctrinal discernment
- Think biblically about contemporary issues

### Doctrinal Focus
- **BFM 2000:** All 18 articles (comprehensive)
- **2nd London Confession:** Focus on distinctively Baptist articles
- **HBC Covenant:** Statement of Biblical Doctrine section

### Weekly Study Breakdown

**Week 1: God's Nature and Attributes**
- Scripture: Exodus 3:14, Isaiah 40, Psalm 90
- Study: BFM 2000 Section II (God), 2nd London Conf. Chapter 2 (God and Trinity)
- Core Questions:
  1. How do God's attributes affect my practical faith?
  2. Why does trinity matter?
  3. How does God's nature distinguish Him from false gods?
- Spiritual Practice: Study one attribute deeply (e.g., God's holiness)
- Habits to Develop: Meditation on God's character daily

**Week 2: Divine Sovereignty and Human Responsibility**
- Scripture: Romans 9-11, Ephesians 1, Acts 4:27-28
- Study: BFM 2000 Section V (God's Purpose of Grace), 2nd London Conf. Chapter 3 (Decree)
- Core Questions:
  1. How do sovereignty and free will coexist?
  2. Does God predestine people to hell?
  3. How does election affect evangelism?
- Spiritual Practice: Personal theology paper on sovereignty
- Habits to Develop: Wrestle with theodicy questions in prayer

**Week 3: The Work of Christ**
- Scripture: Hebrews 9-10, Isaiah 53, Colossians 2:13-15
- Study: BFM 2000 Section II.B, 2nd London Conf. Chapter 8 (Christ)
- Core Questions:
  1. How does substitutionary atonement work?
  2. Why is Jesus's resurrection essential?
  3. What does Christ's ascension mean for today?
- Spiritual Practice: Study theological terms: propitiation, expiation, redemption, reconciliation
- Habits to Develop: Weekly reflection on Christ's sufficiency

**Week 4: Justification, Imputation, and Union with Christ**
- Scripture: Romans 3-5, 2 Corinthians 5:21, Ephesians 1:3-14
- Study: BFM 2000 Section IV.B (Justification), 2nd London Conf. Chapter 11-12
- Core Questions:
  1. How is justification different from sanctification?
  2. What does "imputed righteousness" mean?
  3. How am I "in Christ"?
- Spiritual Practice: Memorize key passages on justification
- Habits to Develop: Daily meditation on one's right standing before God

**Week 5: The Church, Baptism, and Ordinances**
- Scripture: Matthew 16:18, 1 Corinthians 12, Matthew 28:19, 1 Corinthians 11
- Study: BFM 2000 Sections VI-VII, 2nd London Conf. Chapters 26, 29
- Core Questions:
  1. What's the church's relationship to the kingdom?
  2. Why immersion and not sprinkling?
  3. Who should participate in communion?
- Spiritual Practice: Study history of baptismal practice and belief
- Habits to Develop: Theological reflection on church practice

**Week 6: Perseverance and Assurance**
- Scripture: John 10:27-30, Philippians 1:6, Jude, 1 Peter 1:3-5
- Study: BFM 2000 Section V, 2nd London Conf. Chapter 17 (Assurance)
- Core Questions:
  1. Can believers lose their salvation?
  2. What's the difference between assurance and presumption?
  3. How does perseverance differ from perfectionism?
- Spiritual Practice: Write theological statement on security of salvation
- Habits to Develop: Encourage others regarding God's faithfulness

**Week 7: Last Things and Hope**
- Scripture: 1 Thessalonians 4-5, Revelation 19-22, Matthew 24-25
- Study: BFM 2000 Section X (Last Things), 2nd London Conf. Chapter 32
- Core Questions:
  1. What should biblical eschatology look like practically?
  2. How should belief in Christ's return affect my daily living?
  3. What comfort and urgency does the gospel hope provide?
- Spiritual Practice: Study multiple eschatological views fairly; settle your own convictions
- Habits to Develop: Live in light of eternity

**Week 8: Social Order, Stewardship, and Gospel Witness**
- Scripture: Matthew 22:37-40, Romans 12-14, Titus 3:1-8
- Study: BFM 2000 Sections XIII-XVIII
- Core Questions:
  1. How should Christian faith affect my civic engagement?
  2. What does biblical stewardship look like?
  3. How do I live countercultural without compromising love?
- Spiritual Practice: Apply doctrine to 2-3 current cultural issues
- Habits to Develop: Prayerful, thoughtful Christian citizenship

### Assessment
- Complete systematic theology survey
- Articulate personal doctrinal convictions on key matters
- Demonstrate ability to think biblically about cultural issues
- Ready for Phase 6.5 or Phase 7 (track selection)
- Commit to next phase

### Core Objectives
- Understand biblical womanhood

- Prepare for women's ministry leadership roles

- Spiritual Practice: Identify personal spiritual vulnerabilities
- Habits to Develop: Accountability for thought life and media consumption

**Week 6: Discipling Other Women - From Heart to Practice**

**Week 7: Leading Women's Ministry - Vision and Execution**

  1. What vision should guide women's ministry?
  2. How do I create safe space for women to grow?

- Spiritual Practice: Write a vision statement for women's ministry
- Habits to Develop: Collaborate with church leadership on initiatives

**Week 8: Legacy - Investing in Generations**
- Scripture: 2 Timothy 1:5, Proverbs 22:6, Deuteronomy 6:4-9
- Core Questions:
  1. What spiritual legacy am I building?
  2. How do I model faith to the next generation?
  3. What's my role in church renewal?
- Spiritual Practice: Write a personal legacy statement
- Habits to Develop: Identify cross-generational mentoring opportunities

- Articulate biblical vision for Christian womanhood

- Ready for Phase 7 (Women's Ministry Leader)

**Duration:** Ongoing | **Target Audience:** Formally recognized leaders

- Lead women's small groups or Bible studies
- Mentor and disciple individual women
- Plan and execute women's ministry events
- Counsel and provide pastoral care to women in the church
- Collaborate with church leadership on women's spiritual formation

**Ongoing Study & Development:**
- Quarterly advanced teaching/training
- Monthly leaders' roundtable
- Annual women's ministry conference attendance
- Continued theological reading and growth
- Accountability with senior leadership

**Leadership Areas:**
- Women's Bible studies and discipleship groups
- Mentorship and spiritual direction

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Flexibility and Personalization
- Phases are not strictly time-bound; individuals progress based on readiness, not calendar
- Some may complete phases in shorter timeframe; others may need extended time
- Disciplers adapt study to individual spiritual needs
- Circumstances may accelerate (e.g., ready-made leaders joining church) or delay progression

### Technology Platform Integration
- Each phase has specific study content in discipleship platform
- Disciplees work through weekly reflections
- Disciplers assign custom materials as needed
- Progress tracked and visible to discipler
- Discipler-disciplee messaging integrated into platform
- Phase completion marked, enabling advancement

### Small Group and Class-Based Study
- Some phases may be studied in groups (e.g., Phase 3 membership class)
- Other phases require individual discipleship (e.g., Phase 5 leadership development)
- Women's/Men's ministry tracks conducted in gender-specific contexts
- Some content suitable for evening classes or Sunday school
- Discipler has flexibility in delivery method

### Leadership Development Emphasis
- Phases 5-10 specifically designed to identify and develop leaders
- Intentional pipeline from conversion to church office
- Multiple leadership tracks accommodating different callings
- Succession planning built into Phase 10
- Emphasis on multiplication and multiplication of leaders
